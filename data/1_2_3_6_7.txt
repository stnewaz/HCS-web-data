Medical emergencies range in severity and may include: cardiac arrest, serious falls, signs of illness, exposure to hazardous material, and more.

In an emergency, call 911. Additionally, Campus Security provides urgent assistance and first aid 24/7 at all campuses: 778.782.4500. For more, review emergency information and procedures.

Visit our cardiac arrest page for specific procedures, AED maps, and resources.

Visit the Naloxone page for specific procedures for a suspected overdose or toxic drug poisoning, Naloxone cabinet maps, and additional resources. More information on overdose prevention and harm reduction is located on the Health and Counselling - Overdose Prevention and Response page.

For minor injuries, call Campus Security, or if an injured person can walk, proceed to the nearest first aid facility below:


A first aid room is available for treatment of injured/ill persons in the Safety & Risk Services office in Discovery 1. Campus Security Level 2 FAAs have access to the first aid room after hours. 

Surrey campus has a first aid room available for treatment of injured/ill persons in Room 239, Mezzanine Level/Grand Hall.

During regular working hours (8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.) the first aid room is supported by Level 2 SFU FAAs and Security level 2 FAAs. It is equipped with the appropriate level 2 first aid equipment and supplies. 

After regular working hours, the first aid room is available to Security level 2 FAAs. 


Report all health and safety incidents online and to your supervisor within 24 hours of the incidents occurrence.
