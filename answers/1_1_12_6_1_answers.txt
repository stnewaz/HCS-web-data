1.
Research employees are a valuable part of the university's ability to attract world-class researchers and deliver leading-edge research. They conduct and/or support research and are employed by the university, receiving rights and benefits similar to other SFU employee groups.

2.
Research assistants and support roles are employed directly by the university as a result of the Research Personnel Initiative (RPI), rather than being employed directly by Principal Investigators.

3.
The Research Personnel Initiative (RPI) is a program that employs research assistant and research support roles directly by the university, rather than by Principal Investigators. This initiative ensures that these employees receive rights and benefits similar to other SFU employee groups.

4.
Research employees receive rights and benefits similar to other SFU employee groups.

5.
Information available for appointing research employees includes:

- Considerations when planning to employ someone to conduct or support research.
- Steps to follow for appointing, re-appointing, extending, or terminating an appointment.
- Procedures for changing the funding source or correcting previously submitted information.
- Pointers for compliance with employment standards while supervising a research employee.

6.
The steps to follow when re-appointing or extending a research employee's appointment include:

1. Consider the reasons for re-appointment or extension.
2. Review the employee's performance and contributions.
3. Ensure compliance with employment standards.
4. Follow the procedures for the re-appointment process as outlined by the university.
5. Submit necessary documentation to formalize the re-appointment or extension.
6. Communicate clearly with the research employee about the decision and any changes to their role or funding source.

7.
To change the funding source for a research employee, follow the procedures outlined for correcting previously submitted information. Ensure compliance with employment standards while supervising the research employee. For specific steps, consult the relevant sections of the provided documentation or contact the Research Personnel Initiative team for guidance.

8.
The procedures for terminating a research employee's appointment include the following steps: 

1. Follow the guidelines for ending an appointment as outlined on the provided site.
2. Ensure compliance with employment standards during the termination process.
3. If necessary, change the funding source or correct any previously submitted information related to the appointment.
4. Communicate the termination clearly and provide any required documentation or notifications as per university policies.

9.
To ensure compliance with employment standards while supervising a research employee, consider the following steps:

1. Familiarize yourself with the relevant employment standards and regulations.
2. Follow the guidelines provided for appointing, re-appointing, extending, or terminating appointments.
3. Ensure proper documentation and processes are in place when changing funding sources or correcting submitted information.
4. Maintain open communication with the research employee regarding their rights and benefits.
5. Regularly review and adhere to university policies related to employment and supervision of research employees.
