5. Development of the Global Student Centre (GSC) to support student well-being and foster inclusivity, social connectedness, and community.
6. The FHS Student Commons, utilizing the Well-being through Physical Spaces framework for multi-functional design that accommodates individual study, group work, presentations, and research conferences.

7.
SFU Health Promotion contributes to the design of student spaces by providing resources and guidance based on the Principles for Well-being through Physical Spaces. They collaborate with various partners to enhance physical spaces on campus, ensuring that designs support well-being, inclusivity, social connectedness, and functionality. They consult with departments during planning stages to incorporate feedback and suggestions, aiming to create engaging and vibrant community spaces that are well-utilized by students.

8.
The benefits of the FHS Student Commons in terms of study and collaboration include:

1. Enhanced accessibility and functionality of the open space layout.
2. Support for individual study, group work, presentations, and research conferences.
3. Positive feedback from students indicating increased happiness and effectiveness in studying.
4. Encouragement for students to spend more time at school and socialize.

Academic departments and faculties strongly influence students’ experiences at university and provide a core setting for enhancing student well-being.

10.
Graduate students face many academic, research, and life commitments. Teaching Assistants (TAs) and Tutor Markers (TMs) manage a unique university experience by balancing their roles as both students and teachers. Cultivating capacity for resilience and skill building is important to manage commitments and adversity.

11.
The Teaching and Learning Centre supports well-being in learning environments by partnering with instructional staff to create conditions for well-being within those environments.
