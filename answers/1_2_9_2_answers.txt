The purpose of the "8 Ways to Build Resilience" video series is to educate viewers about aspects of resilience and provide activities to follow along with, based on the concept of Bouncing Forward.

2.
The six building blocks of cultivating resilience are: social connection, self-awareness, shifting perspective, goal setting, solving problems, and self-compassion.

3.
Social connection contributes to resilience by fostering quality relationships, which are strong predictors of a healthy and happy life, as shown by a Harvard study. These connections provide support, enhance emotional well-being, and help individuals navigate challenges more effectively.

4.
Close relationships are the strongest predictors of a healthy and happy life.

5.
Self-awareness entails noticing and understanding your thoughts, emotions, and behaviors, and understanding how they affect both you and those around you. It includes knowing your strengths, recognizing and naming emotions, and practicing mindfulness.

10.
Shifting perspective is important in resilience because our perception of events can alter our feelings about them. The lens through which we view the world significantly impacts our experiences in life. One way to shift perspective is by practicing gratitude.

11.
Practicing gratitude can help shift perspective by altering our feelings around events and enhancing our overall experience of life. It encourages a positive lens through which we view the world, contributing to resilience and well-being.

12.
Setting goals gives you long-term vision and short-term motivations, and achieving goals builds your self-confidence. Goals can help you during times of adversity by offering a roadmap out of challenging situations.

13.
SMART goal setting refers to a method of setting goals that are Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.

14.
Problem-solving skills can help during adversity by allowing individuals to overcome or navigate around roadblocks. Research shows that grit, which includes perseverance in the face of adversity, is a key component of success.

15.
Grit is perseverance, even in the face of adversity. It is important for success because it helps individuals overcome or navigate around roadblocks and contributes to achieving long-term goals.

16.
To find balance when feeling overwhelmed, one can build grit and utilize problem-solving skills to navigate challenges.

17.
Self-compassion is the practice of treating yourself compassionately in situations where you might perceive inadequacy, failure, or general suffering. It is beneficial because research shows that self-compassionate individuals suffer less and thrive more.

18.
Imposter Syndrome is the feeling of being a fraud, doubting one's achievements, and feeling undeserving of success. It can be overcome by practicing self-compassion, recognizing and addressing these feelings, and understanding that many others experience similar doubts. Engaging with supportive peers and seeking professional guidance can also help in overcoming these feelings.
