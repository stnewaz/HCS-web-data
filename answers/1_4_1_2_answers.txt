Academic departments and faculties influence student well-being by providing a core setting for enhancing student experiences and well-being. They play a role in shaping policies that can either alleviate or contribute to stress and anxiety, and they offer resources, services, and supports that facilitate access to well-being initiatives.

2.
Policy has a profound ability to impact student well-being by potentially causing undue stress, anxiety, or confusion, or by facilitating access to resources, services, and supports.

4.
The Healthy Campus Community initiative at SFU is an award-winning program focused on enhancing student well-being through partnerships between the Health Promotion team and various faculties, including the Faculty of Applied Sciences and the Faculty of Health Sciences. It involves implementing strategies such as a student wellness survey, wellness curriculum, and experiential learning courses for Masters of Public Health students to develop solutions that promote student health and wellness.
