6.
The most common short-term side effects of benzodiazepines can include: drowsiness, dizziness, confusion, and impaired coordination.

7.
The long-term effects of benzodiazepines can include physical dependence, withdrawal symptoms, and an increased risk of developing a substance use disorder.

8.
It is important to discuss benzodiazepine use with a healthcare provider to make a plan to help reduce the risk of negative side effects, to manage the risk of developing a substance use disorder and dependence, and to ensure safe usage, especially regarding tolerance and potential overdose risks.

9.
Developing tolerance means that, over time, you need more of the drug to get the same effect.

10.
Stopping benzodiazepines for a few days can lead to a loss of tolerance, which increases the chances of overdose when restarting the medication at the same dose.

11.
Mixing benzodiazepines with alcohol or opioids is dangerous because it increases the risk of overdose due to their combined sedative properties.
