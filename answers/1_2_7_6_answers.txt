1.
D&WD is a Therapeutically-Applied Tabletop Roleplaying Game Group that offers a term-long counselling program where participants engage in a short Dungeons & Dragons campaign facilitated by a Registered Clinical Counsellor. It aims to support students with social functioning difficulties, such as social anxiety or neurodivergence.

2.
David Lindskoog, a Registered Clinical Counsellor.

3.
The purpose of the D&WD group is to provide therapeutic support for students facing difficulties with social functioning, such as social anxiety, neurodivergence, trouble initiating or maintaining relationships, and loneliness, through a Dungeons & Dragons campaign facilitated by a Registered Clinical Counsellor.

4.
Students having difficulty with social functioning, such as social anxiety, neurodivergence, trouble initiating or maintaining relationships, and loneliness.

5.
No, prior knowledge of D&D is necessary.

6.
Participants can expect to experience, on average, about a 20% improvement in their social anxiety symptoms.
