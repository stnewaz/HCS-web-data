6.
1. Call for emergency help (e.g., 911).
2. Place the person on their back on a firm surface.
3. Kneel beside the person.
4. Place the heel of one hand on the center of the chest, and the other hand on top.
5. Interlock your fingers and keep your elbows straight.
6. Push hard and fast in the center of the chest at a rate of 100 to 120 compressions per minute.
7. Continue compressions until emergency help arrives or an AED is available.

An AED assists during a cardiac arrest by providing voice prompts that instruct the user on how to use the device and what steps to take. It is easy to use and can significantly increase the chances of survival.
