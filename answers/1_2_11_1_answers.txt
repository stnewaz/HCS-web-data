Fentanyl is a powerful pain medication that is an opioid, similar to morphine, codeine, oxycodone, and methadone. It is most often prescribed as a slow-release patch for people with long-term, severe pain. When used in this way, it can be very effective and safe.

17.
Fentanyl is much stronger than most other opioids—up to 100 times stronger than morphine—and is very dangerous if misused. Even a small amount can cause an overdose and death. Street fentanyl may be swallowed, smoked, snorted, or injected without users realizing it is contaminated with fentanyl, leading to overdoses. When someone overdoses on fentanyl, they may become sleepy, have slow and shallow breathing, and exhibit symptoms such as limp body, pale or clammy face, weak or slow pulse, and blue or purple lips and fingertips.
