The main focus of student mental health initiatives at SFU is to enhance student well-being, mental health, and health equity through collaborative action, creating supportive systems, cultures, curriculum, and programs.

2.
SFU aims to support student well-being and health equity by:

1. Creating systems, cultures, curriculum, and programs that enhance student well-being and mental health.
2. Collaborating across the institution to advance mental health, health equity, and well-being.
3. Infusing health into everyday operations and academic mandates to foster compassion, equity, and social justice.
4. Implementing a holistic approach that encompasses diverse experiences and perspectives on health and well-being.
5. Centering student voice in decision-making and program design.
6. Promoting systemic action to embed well-being across individual and organizational levels.
7. Adopting an asset-based approach to celebrate progress and exemplary actions for well-being.
8. Weaving principles of social justice, accessibility, and equity into all health promotion actions.
9. Committing to decolonization, reconciliation, and Indigenization in practices and programs.
10. Utilizing evidence-informed and reflexive practices for continuous learning and growth.
11. Recognizing the interconnectedness of community health and ecological sustainability in health promotion activities.
12. Striving for diverse perspectives to shape policies and organizational structures impacting student well-being.
13. Developing physical spaces that support student inclusion and thriving.
14. Embedding well-being in learning environments through collaboration with academic units.
15. Creating cultures of connection and belonging for equity-deserving students.
16. Providing comprehensive mental health and well-being education.
17. Offering coordinated, accessible, and culturally appropriate support services to students.

13.
HCS offers coordinated, accessible, and culturally appropriate support that includes:

1. Warm hand-offs to various support options.
2. An interdisciplinary team of professionals addressing both mental and physical health needs.
3. A stepped care model prioritizing student needs.
4. Timely management of critical incidents.
5. Early intervention services.
6. Holistic and comprehensive well-being support, including trauma-informed and integrated case management or advising for issues like housing, immigration, finances, disabilities, and academics.

18.
SFU's approach to mental health aligns with national standards by implementing a Student Mental Health & Well-being Framework that outlines evidence-informed strategies to enhance student mental health and well-being. This framework incorporates recommendations from the National Standard for Post-Secondary Student Mental Health & Well-being and includes commitments to policies and organizational systems, creating physical spaces for well-being, embedding well-being in learning environments, promoting equity, connection, and inclusion, enhancing mental health and well-being literacy, and providing support services. The framework serves as a planning tool that guides actions and initiatives to improve student well-being and health equity at SFU.

19.
Evidence-informed practice is central to SFU's mental health strategy as it involves continual learning, reflexive practice, innovation, and growth. Diverse research, evidence, evaluation, lived experience, and various sources of knowledge are utilized to inform strategies aimed at enhancing student mental health and well-being.
