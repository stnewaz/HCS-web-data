The "Well-being through the Supervisory Process: Graduate Student Guide for Action" provides resources for graduate students to advocate for their needs within the supervisory process and understand available campus resources.

7.
The guides address the well-being of graduate students by providing frameworks and conditions that focus on the impact of the supervisory process on their physical, mental, and social well-being. The Faculty Guide outlines 7 conditions informed by research and best practices to enhance the learning environment, while the Graduate Student Guide offers 5 conditions aimed at optimizing the supervisory experience and supporting student advocacy for their needs. Both guides incorporate input from faculty and graduate students to ensure relevance and effectiveness in promoting well-being.
