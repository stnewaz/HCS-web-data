December 6 was established as the National Day of Remembrance and Action on Violence against Women in Canada to commemorate the anniversary of the murder of 14 women at l'École Polytechnique de Montréal in 1989, which was a violent misogynist act. The day serves as a time to reflect on and take action against gender-based violence.
