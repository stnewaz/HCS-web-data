1.
Self care is the act of prioritizing one's physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual wellbeing. It is important for Survivors of sexual violence because it helps them navigate the impacts of trauma and supports their healing journey. Practicing self care can look different for each person and can aid in recovery by addressing personal needs and establishing boundaries.

4.
Surrounding yourself with a supportive community is important during the healing process because it provides empathy, compassion, and a space to hear and learn from others facing similar challenges. Support groups dedicated to Survivors of sexual violence can significantly aid in the healing process.

5.
- It's okay to not want to be touched without consent (or even at all).
- It's okay to leave spaces that don't feel safe.
- It's okay to tell your partner(s) what your limits are during any sexual activity.
- Know and enforce your boundaries, as they are crucial to your healing journey.
- You do not have to disclose that you are a Survivor to anyone.
- You are not required to make excuses for your boundaries.
- Respecting your boundaries applies to how you communicate about your trauma.

6.
Being kind to yourself during your healing journey means recognizing that healing is not linear and takes time. It involves having grace for the space you're in, understanding that everyone experiences trauma differently, and not comparing your progress to others. It's important to acknowledge your feelings and identity on your own terms, and to set your own rules for self care without the pressure of external standards.
