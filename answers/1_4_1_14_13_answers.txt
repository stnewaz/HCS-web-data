3.
To encourage students to share their contact information with a classmate, have them identify a “backup person” whose notes they can borrow or who will pick up handouts if they miss class. Instruct them to swap first names and contact info (phone, email). Emphasize that using first names is less threatening and easier to remember. Additionally, lighten the activity by saying, “don’t worry, you don’t have to marry this person!”

4.
A good way to engage students with course-related questions is to form pairs and pose a simple question related to the material. Students can introduce themselves, discuss the question briefly, and then write their answers on a piece of paper to hand in anonymously. Additionally, in larger groups, students can identify stressors related to being a university student and brainstorm solutions, which can be noted by one member of the group. This encourages both social interaction and engagement with the course content.
