4.
To create a positive classroom culture through student feedback, you can:

1. Invite students to share information about themselves and provide feedback, fostering an inclusive environment.
2. Make adjustments to course content and assessments, such as changing the final exam based on students’ experiences from the mid-term, which promotes flexibility.
3. Implement a student-centered approach that caters to different learning preferences, enhancing the instructor-student relationship.

5.
The changes made to the final exam based on student experiences include adjusting the exam format or content to reflect feedback received from students after the mid-term exam. This flexibility aims to better align the assessment with student learning and needs.

6.
In a synchronous Faculty of Health Sciences research methods class, students were given time during the first lecture and in the middle of the term to complete activities designed to enhance well-being. These activities allowed students to share information about themselves and provide feedback, contributing to a positive classroom culture.

7.
The described activities can be adapted to other teaching platforms by incorporating opportunities for students to share information about themselves and provide feedback, which can help build a positive classroom culture. Additionally, adjustments to course content and assessments, such as modifying the final exam based on mid-term experiences, can be implemented across various platforms to enhance flexibility and cater to different learning preferences.
