Garden therapy, also known as horticulture therapy, is a therapeutic practice that involves engaging with plants to manage stress and promote mental and physical well-being. It allows participants to connect with nature through activities such as planting, maintaining, and harvesting, while also benefiting from the guidance of a professional therapist.

2.
Horticultural therapy helps manage stress by allowing participants to interact with plants, which boosts moods and reduces stress. It provides comfort and support through horticultural activities and connects individuals with nature. The full effects are best achieved with the guidance of a professional therapist, combining planting, maintaining, and harvesting with therapy concepts that promote personal growth and resiliency.

3.
Activities involved in horticulture therapy include checking soil temperature, watering plants, fertilizing, pruning dried or dead leaves, and providing protection from the elements and pests.
