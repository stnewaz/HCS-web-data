SFU manages hazardous materials through established programs such as the Biosafety, Radiation Safety, and Chemical Safety Programs, which regulate the safe use, storage, and disposal of these materials. Additionally, SFU has a Hazardous Materials Emergency Response Plan in place for responding to incidents, assessing risks, assigning responsibilities, and outlining necessary steps during emergencies.

3. If in doubt about a minor spill, evacuate and call for assistance.
4. For major spills that present an immediate threat, notify everyone around and immediately evacuate using the further exit from the spill.

6. If necessary, evacuate the entire building by pulling the fire alarm and calling 911.

9.
SFU ensures compliance with WorkSafeBC regulations by having a Hazardous Materials Emergency Response Plan that complies with Part 5 of WorkSafeBC's Occupational Health and Safety Regulation. This plan outlines the requirements for emergency planning related to hazardous substances and was recently updated to incorporate regulatory amendments. Additionally, the laboratory safety programs support education, training, and establish program-specific requirements for the safe use, storage, and disposal of hazardous materials.

10.
The Laboratory Power Outages Guide includes suggested actions to take before, during, and after a power outage impacting laboratory operations.

11.
Before a power outage:
- Develop a plan to protect laboratory equipment, materials, and research.
- Prepare using the Laboratory Power Outages Guide and Infosheet.

During a power outage:
- Follow the actions outlined in the Laboratory Power Outages Guide and Infosheet.

After a power outage:
- Assess the condition of equipment and materials.
- Implement any necessary recovery procedures as indicated in the guide.
