To implement the 9 Principles of Universal Design in your classroom, consider the following strategies:

1. **Equitable Use**: Ensure that your teaching methods are accessible to all students, providing the same means of use for everyone.
   
2. **Flexibility in Use**: Allow for a variety of ways for students to engage with the material, accommodating different learning styles and preferences.

3. **Simple and Intuitive Use**: Make sure that your instructions and materials are easy to understand and navigate.

4. **Perceptible Information**: Present information in multiple formats (e.g., visual, auditory) to ensure all students can access the content.

5. **Tolerance for Error**: Design your assessments and activities to minimize the consequences of mistakes, allowing for a safe learning environment.

6. **Low Physical Effort**: Reduce the physical demands on students by providing accessible resources and minimizing unnecessary tasks.

7. **Size and Space for Approach and Use**: Ensure that your classroom setup accommodates all students, including those with mobility challenges.

8. **Community of Learners**: Foster a supportive environment where students can collaborate and learn from each other.

9. **Feedback**: Provide timely and constructive feedback to help students understand their progress and areas for improvement. 

Consider adapting these principles both in-person and in online platforms like Canvas.
