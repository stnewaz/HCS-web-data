Traveling "roped up" means moving as a group, where individuals are connected by a rope, such as in mountaineering. It emphasizes the need for group cohesion, as each person's pace, balance, and missteps affect the entire group. For the system to work effectively, everyone must find a shared rhythm together.

Referring to pronouns as "preferred" can be problematic because it implies that using the correct pronouns is a matter of personal preference rather than a fundamental aspect of a person's identity. This framing can undermine the validity of a person's gender identity and can make it seem optional rather than essential for respect and recognition. Additionally, it may suggest that there are acceptable or unacceptable ways to refer to someone, which can contribute to misunderstanding and misgendering.

1. Implementing comprehensive accessibility features throughout the entire space, not just limited adjustments like adding gender options.
2. Ensuring that all changes are thoughtfully planned and executed, considering the overall experience of all employees.
3. Actively involving and consulting with trans and non-binary individuals during the planning and implementation of inclusivity measures.
4. Creating an environment where the unique needs of all genders are recognized and addressed in policies, practices, and benefits.
5. Continuously evaluating and improving inclusivity efforts based on feedback and evolving best practices.
