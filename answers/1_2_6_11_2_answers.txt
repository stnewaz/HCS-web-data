1.
The SVSPO offers free, confidential support for students who have experienced sexualized violence, witnessed it, or are providing support to someone else. Support is available in-person on any of SFU's three campuses or by phone, email, text, or Zoom. They assist with medical care, including access to doctors and counselors at SFU Health and Counselling Services, and provide transportation and accompaniment to hospitals for specialized support and medical treatment. They also create guides for culturally relevant support services for BIPOC and Indigenous individuals impacted by sexualized violence.

6.
A forensic exam is a medical examination conducted after a sexual assault to collect DNA and other evidence. Someone might want to have one to gather evidence that can be used if they wish to report the sexual assault to the police. It should be completed at a local hospital within seven days of the assault.

7.
The evidence collected can be securely stored for up to one year.

The Sexual Violence Support & Prevention Office (SVSPO) has created a guide to SFU and community services that offer culturally relevant support services for BIPOC (Black, Indigenous and People of Colour) who have been affected by sexualized violence. Additionally, there is a collaboration with the Indigenous Student Centre to provide a guide for Indigenous people impacted by gender-based or sexualized violence.
