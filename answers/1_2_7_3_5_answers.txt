1.
SFU350 is a student-led club at Simon Fraser University that organizes for climate justice, mobilizes for systemic change, and engages the SFU community in climate action.

2.
SFU350 operates on the unceded, traditional, and ancestral Coast Salish territories of the xʷməθkʷəy̓əm (Musqueam), Sḵwx̱wú7mesh (Squamish), səl̓ilwətaɁɬ (Tsleil-Waututh), Katzie, and kʷikʷəƛ̓əm (Kwikwetlem) Nations.

5.
The Trans Mountain Pipeline Expansion (TMX) is a project that aims to expand an existing pipeline, which is situated dangerously close to Burnaby Mountain. It poses significant environmental and social impacts, threatening the safety of commuters, residents, and wildlife in the area.

6.
The safety concerns related to the Trans Mountain Pipeline Expansion (TMX) include:

1. It is situated dangerously close to Burnaby Mountain.
2. It threatens the safety of thousands of daily commuters and residents.
3. It poses risks to wildlife.
4. It has devastating environmental and social impacts.

7.
The DivestSFU campaign involved lobbying SFU to divest its endowment fund from fossil fuels and invest in renewable energy and low-carbon solutions. It became one of seven demands in the Climate Emergency Declaration letter to SFU. In November 2021, SFU announced its commitment to complete divestment from fossil fuels by 2025.

15.
SFU350 acknowledges Indigenous sovereignty by respectfully recognizing that they are working on the unceded, traditional, and ancestral territories of the xʷməθkʷəy̓əm (Musqueam), Sḵwx̱wú7mesh (Squamish), səl̓ilwətaɁɬ (Tsleil-Waututh), Katzie, and kʷikʷəƛ̓əm (Kwikwetlem) Nations. They emphasize that this land was never surrendered and center their movement around Indigenous rights and sovereignty.

16.
The Climate Coalition organizes sessions specifically for students to ensure their voices are heard, ideas are shared, and connections are made regarding the climate emergency. They aim to create a safe space for all SFU students to share their thoughts, concerns, and proposals on how SFU can lead in the fight against climate change.

17.
Climate justice is a term used for framing global warming as an ethical, social, and political issue, rather than one that is purely environmental or physical in nature. It is important to SFU350 because the club aims to engage the SFU community in climate action and mobilize for systemic change in solidarity with intersecting movements, emphasizing the need to address the ethical and social dimensions of climate change alongside environmental concerns.
