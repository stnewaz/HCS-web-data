The creation of the InterroBang game was inspired by a question about how to better support students from international pathways in learning about relationships and boundaries in an intercultural Canadian context. It was also influenced by feedback and survey data from students, and was developed in response to their interests in exploring topics related to healthy relationships, consent, culture, and boundaries in a fun and engaging manner.

10.
Students were involved in the development of the game by suggesting questions related to healthy relationships, consent, culture, and boundaries during brainstorming sessions. Their feedback during creative workshops helped refine the game questions and levels, ensuring the language used was accessible and reflected their own words. Additionally, students tested the game play and provided meaningful insights that shaped its development.

11.
Students' feedback during the game testing sparked meaningful conversations on how culture and family shape relationships. Their ideas helped refine the game questions and levels into simple categories and accessible language, using their own words.
