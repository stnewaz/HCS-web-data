1.
The Global Student Centre offers the following types of events for international students at SFU:

1. Social events to foster connections among international students.
2. Welcome Day activities for families of international students.
3. International Student Welcome Socials.
4. Service Canada sessions for obtaining SIN.
5. Creative Collective for socializing and creative activities.
6. Cultural events featuring food and activities.
7. Halloween social event.
8. Friendship bracelet-making and relaxation events.
9. Food distribution to support students facing food insecurity.
10. English Conversation Circles for practicing speaking skills.
11. Networking events for students in the Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences.
12. Information sessions about co-op opportunities and work permits.
13. TA support sessions for teaching assistants.

2.
All SFU students, including international undergraduate and graduate students, study abroad and exchange students, and any newcomer students from outside of Canada are eligible to participate in the GSC events.

3.
The purpose of the Welcome Day activities at SFU is to help international students connect with each other, create a supportive community, and kick off the new semester with social events that celebrate intercultural diversity and provide information about available resources and services.

4.
The Creative Collective event is held from 1:30 pm to 3:30 pm at the Global Student Centre (AQ 2013).

5.
No, all events at the Global Student Centre are free.

6.
Students can expect a fun Halloween social with costumes, candy, crafts, laughter, and light snacks provided. Everyone is welcome, and no special items need to be brought—just festive spirit.

7.
The activities planned for the friendship bracelet-making event include making friendship bracelets, watching or singing along to The Eras Tour concert movie, playing games, and enjoying snacks.

8.
Students can benefit from the English Conversation Circles by practicing and improving their English speaking skills, engaging in conversations on various topics, meeting new people, and enhancing their language abilities in a relaxed and supportive environment. All levels are welcome to participate.

10.
Resources available for teaching assistants at the Global Student Centre include support with teaching strategies, managing tutorials, dealing with tricky situations, balancing workload, grading and giving feedback, and time management. Additionally, participants can meet peers, build community, and expand their TA toolkit.
