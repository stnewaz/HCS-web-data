3.
Goal setting and accountability help with procrastination by providing a clear framework for what needs to be accomplished, making tasks more manageable. Setting specific, achievable goals allows individuals to focus on smaller steps rather than feeling overwhelmed by larger tasks. Accountability, whether through self-monitoring or sharing goals with others, creates a sense of responsibility, encouraging individuals to follow through and complete tasks in a timely manner.

The Pomodoro technique is a time management method that involves breaking work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks. This technique helps maintain focus and productivity by encouraging regular breaks to refresh the mind.

The "5-minute technique" involves starting a task by committing to work on it for just five minutes. This approach helps to overcome the initial resistance to starting a task, making it easier to continue working beyond the initial time commitment.

10.
Perfectionism contributes to procrastination by creating unrealistic standards and fear of failure, leading individuals to avoid starting or completing tasks. Perfectionists may struggle with decision-making and feel overwhelmed by the desire for flawless results, which can result in delays and avoidance of tasks altogether.
