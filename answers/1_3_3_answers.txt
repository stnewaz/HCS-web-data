The purpose of the Tools for Well-being in the Classroom (TWC) initiative is to support student learning and well-being by encouraging faculty members, instructors, and TAs/TMs to implement quick strategies in their classes and evaluate their impact through student participation in surveys. Additionally, it aims to expand the network of individuals interested in exploring strategies that promote well-being in learning environments.

Well-being can impact student success in post-secondary education by creating conditions that support student learning, engagement, and retention. Improved well-being can lead to reduced anxiety and stress, which enhances the overall classroom environment and facilitates better learning outcomes. Additionally, a supportive network among faculty, instructors, and TAs/TMs can foster a sense of community, encouraging students to share and engage more deeply, ultimately contributing to their academic success.
