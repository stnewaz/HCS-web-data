1.
The Good Samaritan Drug Overdose Act is a law that provides legal protection for individuals who seek emergency help during an overdose situation. It encourages Canadians to call 911 or their local emergency number to save a life during an overdose. The Act applies to anyone seeking emergency support during an overdose, including the person experiencing an overdose, and protects those who stay or leave the scene before help arrives.

2.
The Good Samaritan Drug Overdose Act became law on May 4, 2017.

3.
The Good Samaritan Drug Overdose Act aims to encourage Canadians to save a life during an overdose situation by providing legal protection for individuals who seek emergency help during an overdose. It seeks to reduce the fear of police involvement at overdose events and ensure that people feel safe to call for help.

4.
The Good Samaritan Drug Overdose Act provides legal protection for individuals who seek emergency help during an overdose. It protects the person who calls 911 or their local emergency number, regardless of whether they stay at the scene or leave before help arrives. The Act also protects other witnesses present at the scene when help arrives.

5.
The Good Samaritan Drug Overdose Act applies to anyone seeking emergency support during an overdose, including the person experiencing an overdose. It protects the person who seeks help, whether they stay or leave from the overdose scene before help arrives, as well as anyone else who is at the scene when help arrives.

6.
The Good Samaritan Drug Overdose Act provides legal protection for individuals who:

1. Seek emergency help during an overdose.
2. Call 911 or their local emergency number for help.
3. Stay at the scene or leave before help arrives.
4. Are present at the scene when help arrives.

7.
The Act does not provide legal protection against more serious offences.

8.
Staying at the scene is important to help save the life of the person experiencing an overdose.
