2.
Gaming can interfere with personal or academic goals by:

1. Consuming excessive time that could be spent on studying or completing assignments.
2. Leading to neglect of responsibilities and personal relationships.
3. Causing distraction and difficulty focusing on tasks.
4. Contributing to increased stress and anxiety if gaming becomes a source of escapism. 
5. Resulting in poor sleep patterns due to late-night gaming sessions, affecting overall health and productivity.

3.
1. Set time limits for gaming sessions.
2. Schedule regular breaks during gaming.
3. Prioritize personal and academic responsibilities over gaming.
4. Engage in physical activities or hobbies outside of gaming.
5. Practice mindfulness and self-reflection regarding gaming habits.
6. Establish a healthy gaming routine that incorporates social interactions.
7. Seek support from counselors or peers if gaming habits become concerning.

4.
Healthy gaming practices can improve mental health symptoms by promoting social connections, providing a constructive outlet for stress, enhancing cognitive skills, and offering a sense of achievement and purpose. Additionally, they can help individuals establish routines, manage emotions, and develop problem-solving skills.

5.
The topics to be covered in the workshop on healthy and unhealthy gaming will include:

1. The effects of gaming habits on well-being.
2. Strategies for balancing gaming with personal and academic goals.
3. Healthy gaming practices that may improve mental health symptoms.
4. Concerns related to video game habits.
