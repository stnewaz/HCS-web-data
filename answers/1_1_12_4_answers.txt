Primary Insurance and Secondary Insurance.

2.
Primary medical insurance covers services that require medical attention, such as visits to doctors, hospital stays, and diagnostic medical testing. It is legally required for all residents of British Columbia, including international students, and is provided through the BC Medical Services Plan (MSP).

3.
To apply for BC Medical Services Plan (MSP), you can complete the MSP Application online or by mail. You may also complete the MSP Application Enrolment form and mail it directly to BC MSP. If you are eligible, you can apply as soon as you arrive in BC.

4.
New students should apply for the BC Medical Services Plan (MSP) as soon as they arrive in BC. During the 3-month waiting period for BC MSP coverage, they should enroll in the guard.me short-term medical insurance plan, which will automatically cover them for their first term at SFU. If they have not used the guard.me plan, they may opt out of it.

5.
The waiting period for BC MSP coverage is 2-3 months for new residents.

6.
Guard.me@SFU is a short-term primary medical insurance coverage for students' first term at SFU. It is designed to help students transition while waiting for their BC Medical Services Plan (MSP) coverage.

7.
To opt out of the guard.me@SFU medical insurance plan, you must meet certain criteria, such as having valid coverage through BC Medical Services Plan (MSP) or another Canadian provincial or territorial public health coverage, being under a government-sponsored scholarship program, or being outside of Canada. 

To proceed with the opt-out, visit the opt-out website, select your appropriate student category, and follow the opt-out instructions. For deadlines regarding the opt-out process, consult the provided information. 

If opting out with BC MSP, you need to submit proof of MSP, which must include a photo of your MSP card along with one additional document.

9.
When visiting any of the SFU Health Clinics, print out a copy of your Healthcare Access Card (HAC) from the guard.me@SFU website. The HAC is your proof of insurance containing your coverage information. They will collect a copy for each visit and will directly bill guard.me@SFU on your behalf. No additional claim is required.

For clinics that do not allow direct billing, you will need to pay upfront for the visit. Keep copies of all your payment receipts. To submit a claim, please visit guard.me@SFU to create an online account and upload copies of your receipts.

Yes, you may be eligible for a refund of the unused portion of your insurance if you have not made a claim and there is more than 1 month of coverage remaining when you apply through guard.me@SFU. Your application for a partial reimbursement must be received by guard.me@SFU before the last month of the term. If approved, your partial refund will be applied to your SFU account.

11.
Once you have received your new study permit, you can notify BC MSP by completing an online form and uploading a copy of your new study permit. Please visit the MSP website for more information.

12.
Secondary medical insurance at SFU generally covers vision care, prescription drugs, dental, and extended health care.

13.
To opt out of the SFSS Health and Dental Plan for the first time or if you didn't opt out last year, you need to provide proof that you are covered by an equivalent extended health and/or dental plan during the Change-of-Coverage Period. Please visit their website for more information.

14.
To enroll your dependents in the SFSS Health and Dental Plan, complete an enrollment process and pay an additional fee, over and above your individual fee as a Plan member during the Change-of-Coverage Period. Common law couples are eligible.
