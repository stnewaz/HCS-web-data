The SVSPO offers confidential support and guidance for any member of the SFU and FIC community impacted by sexual violence, including faculty and teaching support staff. They assist individuals who have experienced or witnessed sexual violence or who are providing support to someone else. Additionally, SVSPO case managers are available to provide guidance, resources, and personal support. They also offer free, on-demand workshops and outreach sessions for all members of the SFU and FIC communities.

6.
Content notes (also referred to as “content warnings”) give students advance notice of content that could be distressing for people impacted by sexual violence or other forms of trauma. They can be used in the following ways:

1. General course content: Provide a content note that acknowledges potentially distressing topics and invites students to seek support if needed.
2. Specific readings, classes, or assignments: Include a content note that describes the specific distressing content and offers support options, while also allowing for discussion on alternative pathways to achieve learning goals.

7.
Safeguarding privacy and confidentiality is crucial because it ensures that identifying information is not shared without explicit consent, which helps protect the individual’s dignity and safety. This approach fosters a trusting environment where individuals feel safe to disclose their experiences without fear of further trauma or breach of trust. Additionally, it allows an SVSPO case manager to provide guidance without requiring the disclosure of the student's identity.

8.
Addressing an incident in your classroom or learning environment can involve:

1. Pointing out the behavior or language that does not correspond to the expectations for respectful conduct in the syllabus.
2. Redirecting by inviting the individual who engaged in problematic behavior to follow up with you outside of class time if they have questions.
3. Following up with the class to remind them of support resources available.
4. Consulting with the Office of Student Support, Rights, and Responsibilities, the Centre for Educational Excellence (CEE), and the SVSPO for guidance if unsure about how to address any issues.
