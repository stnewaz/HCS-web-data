6.
Accommodations for students with disabilities during the summer term will be managed as follows:

1. Accommodation notices will continue to be sent to instructors via email starting the week of May 19.
2. Lecture accommodations (e.g., audio recording) will apply to students’ live, online lectures where relevant.
3. Exam notifications/reminders will be sent to instructors once a student has requested accommodations for a specific test.
4. Instructors will oversee the administration of exams for students with straightforward accommodations (e.g., additional time less than or equal to 66%).
5. CAL will oversee the administration of exams for students requiring exceptional accommodations (e.g., additional time greater than 67%, use of a scribe).
6. Extra exam time accommodations will be implemented by instructors/TAs by adjusting the student’s test duration in Canvas or alternative platforms.
7. Students have been asked to submit test booking requests online to CAL, up to one week in advance for midterms/quizzes, and two weeks before finals.
8. Assistance will be provided for implementing accommodations via Zoom when required for students with specific needs.
9. Instructors are encouraged to consult CAL for any issues regarding the implementation of accommodations.

9.
Students with straightforward accommodations (e.g., a standard increment of extra time) will be expected to write exams alongside their classmates and be invigilated (where applicable) by their instructors/TAs.

10.
Instructors should ensure that exams for students requiring unique or demanding accommodations (e.g., use of a scribe or a relatively large increment of extra time) are administered and invigilated by the Centre for Accessible Learning (CAL) via Zoom. They should communicate with CAL to identify and implement appropriate accommodations.

11.
Instructors can adjust test durations in Canvas for students with extra time accommodations by following these steps:

1. Calculate the extra time required based on the student's accommodation.
2. Modify the individual student's test time in Canvas to reflect their adjusted exposure/writing time, which includes both the extra time and any allotted break time.
3. For detailed step-by-step illustrated instructions, refer to the link: [Canvas Extra Time Instructions](http://www.sfu.ca/students/accessible-learning/faculty/resources/canvas-extra-time.html).

12.
If an exam is not administered on Canvas, the following steps should be taken:

1. Adjust the exposure time to reflect the student’s entitlement and inform the student of their adjusted return time for the exam.
2. The student's adjusted exposure/writing time should include both their extra time and any time allotted for breaks.
3. If the exam is scheduled in the evening and the adjustments would result in the student writing past 10 PM, adjust the start time for the CAL registrant to ensure they finish by 10 PM.
4. If accommodations cannot be effectively implemented, explore flexible alternatives with the student’s Disability Access Advisor, such as providing an assignment or paper instead of a time-based exam.

13.
Evening exam schedules for CAL registrants should be adjusted to an earlier start time to ensure they finish by 10:00 PM, as all examinations at SFU are scheduled to end by this time.
