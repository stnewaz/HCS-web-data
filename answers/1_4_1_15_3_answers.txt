1.
The Healthy Campus Community initiative at SFU is a foundational, award-winning initiative by SFU’s Health Promotion team that takes a systemic, institution-wide approach to enhance conditions conducive to well-being. It aims to create healthy settings by ensuring that policies, processes, learning environments, and physical spaces support student mental health and well-being. The initiative has been internationally recognized for its leadership in advancing systemic health promotion within a university context and directly contributes to SFU's Strategy by promoting and protecting student mental health and well-being.

2.
SFU’s Health Promotion team has been recognized internationally for leadership in advancing systemic health promotion within a university context, particularly through the development of the Okanagan Charter. Since its release in 2015, hundreds of post-secondary institutions across 40 different countries have become signatories of the Charter and adopted initiatives prioritizing student health and well-being. The team has also been featured by various organizations and institutions, including EAB and Princeton University, which have referenced their work in online materials.

3.
The Healthy Campus Community initiative takes a systemic, institution-wide approach to enhance conditions that are conducive to well-being. It focuses on creating healthy settings by ensuring that policies, processes, learning environments, physical spaces, and other aspects of the institutional environment are designed to support student mental health and well-being.

4.
The Healthy Campus Community initiative contributes to SFU’s strategic goals by promoting and protecting student mental health and well-being, enhancing conditions conducive to well-being, creating equitable and healthy systems and environments, supporting student retention and success, and aligning with the SFU Strategy, which prioritizes mental health as integral to the institution's core business.

5.
The Okanagan Charter is an international charter for health-promoting universities and colleges that emphasizes the importance of promoting student health and well-being within post-secondary institutions. It was developed in 2015, and SFU's Health Promotion team led the charter development process. Since then, SFU has been recognized as an international leader in this area, and many post-secondary institutions worldwide have adopted initiatives based on the Okanagan Charter to prioritize student health and well-being.

9.
Student mental health is closely tied to academic success and retention, as poor mental health can negatively impact performance, with 84% of students reporting that their mental health interferes with their academic performance. Additionally, factors such as stress, which is often a significant barrier to academic success, have been documented. Retention rates are influenced by student mental health, with well-being being essential for engagement and success in higher education.

10.
The social determinants of health mentioned in the text are non-medical factors, forces, and systems that shape the conditions of daily life, which influence health outcomes. Specific groups that are disproportionately impacted by these determinants include international students and students from equity deserving communities.

11.
The COVID-19 pandemic resulted in greater stressors impacting student mental well-being, particularly for international students and those from equity-deserving communities. It was associated with increased anxiety, social isolation, financial stress, and higher academic loads. Additionally, a significant portion of students reported negative mental health during their studies, with 50% experiencing severe or moderate mental distress and 84% indicating that their mental health interfered with their academic performance.

13.
The Healthy Campus Community initiative aligns with national mental health standards by adhering to the National Standard for Post-secondary Mental Health & Well-being, the Okanagan Charter, and the Scarborough Charter. It emphasizes a systemic, campus-wide approach to create environments that enhance health and well-being, which is consistent with recommendations from the National Standard of Canada for Mental Health and Well-being for Post-Secondary Students. The initiative also integrates current provincial and national efforts to improve well-being in post-secondary institutions, such as those by the Canadian Mental Health Association BC Division’s Healthy Minds | Healthy Campuses and the Centre for Innovation in Campus Mental Health.

14.
The foundational documents that support the Healthy Campus Community initiative are the National Standard for Post-secondary Mental Health & Well-being, the Okanagan Charter, and the Scarborough Charter.
