The Healthy Campus Community initiative at SFU is a program aimed at fostering well-being and supporting campus members to thrive and succeed. It emphasizes the importance of creating a healthy campus community where people, programs, practices, policies, and spaces contribute to health and well-being. The initiative focuses on areas such as learning and working environments, policies and processes, personal growth and development, social connection and community engagement, physical spaces, and services and supports.

A critical incident is typically a traumatic event that creates a strong emotional reaction and may interfere with the ability to manage day-to-day activities, negatively impacting those involved. It should be addressed by creating a supportive community that looks out for its members and responds to critical incidents with empathy and compassion.
