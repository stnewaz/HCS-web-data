Primary Insurance and Secondary Insurance.

2.
To get medical insurance coverage as a current TA/TM/Sessional or Facilitator at SFU, follow these steps:

1. **Enroll in BC Medical Services Plan (MSP)**: Apply for BC MSP as soon as you arrive in BC, as it is the primary medical insurance required under the Medicare Protection Act.

2. **Temporary Coverage**: If you are a new international student, you will be automatically enrolled in guard.me@SFU for your first term to cover the waiting period for MSP.

3. **Opting Out**: If you have proof of MSP coverage, you can opt out of the guard.me@SFU plan by visiting their website and following the opt-out instructions.

4. **Secondary Insurance**: For secondary medical insurance, eligible students receive coverage through the SFSS/GSS Extended Health and Dental Plan. You may also opt into a private extended health plan (Pacific Blue Cross) if you have an active TSSU appointment.

5. **Contact HR**: For detailed information, visit the SFU Human Resources TSSU website or contact them directly for assistance.

3.
Benefits for Research Assistants regarding medical insurance depend on grant holder approval. To be eligible for benefits, the grant holder must provide written authorization to SFU Human Resources stating that the grant will be responsible for 50% of the monthly premiums.

4.
The BC Medical Services Plan (MSP) is a mandatory medical insurance program for residents of British Columbia, including international students. It provides primary medical coverage, which includes services such as visits to doctors, hospital stays, and diagnostic medical testing.

5.
The waiting period for BC MSP coverage is 3 months.

6.
The interim solution for the waiting period before BC MSP coverage starts is a short-term medical insurance plan called guard.me.

7.
To opt out of guard.me@SFU, you must follow these steps:

1. Apply for and receive proof of your BC MSP enrolment (this includes a scanned copy of your valid BC Services Card, a current invoice, or a letter confirming coverage).
2. Visit the guard.me@SFU website and follow the opt-out instructions under the "graduate" category.
3. Ensure you complete the opt-out process before the deadline specified on the website.

8.
To apply for BC MSP, you must:

1. Enrol yourself (and any dependent family members) in the group plan upon arrival through SFU Human Resources.
2. Start the application process as soon as you receive your TSSU appointment.

9.
When visiting any of the SFU Health Clinics, print out a copy of your Healthcare Access Card (HAC) from the guard.me@SFU website. The HAC is your proof of insurance containing your coverage information. They will collect a copy for each visit and will directly bill guard.me@SFU on your behalf. No additional claim is required. 

For clinics that do not allow direct billing, you will need to pay upfront for the visit. Keep copies of all your payment receipts. To submit a claim, please visit guard.me@SFU to create an online account and upload copies of your receipts.

12.
The GSS Health and Dental Plan is a program designed to complement primary medical coverage (i.e., BC MSP). It includes services not covered by primary insurance, such as vision and dental care. Eligible students are automatically covered by and billed for applicable portions of the student plan, except for those entering in the summer term. Note that plan benefits are only accessible within Canada.

13.
You may opt out of the GSS extended health plan if you have existing secondary coverage in BC. To opt out, you will be required to upload proof of coverage on the GSS Health and Dental website during the Change-of-Coverage Period.

Yes, you can have both the GSS/SFSS health and dental plan and the TSSU extended health and dental plan. One benefit is that you can claim eligible medical and dental expenses to both plans and receive a higher amount of reimbursement. However, you will need to compare the two policies to make an informed decision that fits your individual needs.

16.
If you opt out of the GSS/SFSS health and dental plan while having the TSSU plan, you cannot re-enroll in the GSS/SFSS plan for the entire academic year. This means there is a risk of having no secondary medical insurance coverage if your TSSU appointment ends in the middle of the academic year. Additionally, there is a 3-month waiting period for the TSSU dental plan.

17.
You may be eligible for a refund of the unused portion of your insurance if you have not made a claim and there is more than 1 month of coverage remaining when you apply through guard.me@SFU. Your application for a partial reimbursement must be received by guard.me@SFU before the last month of the term (that is, before April 1, August 1, and December 1). If approved, your partial refund will be applied to your SFU account. If you are a TSSU member, additionally, SFU HR will reimburse 85% of the guard.me fee paid to the maximum cost for two persons, processed at the end of the semester. You must notify HR that you are covered by guard.me for reimbursement to occur.
