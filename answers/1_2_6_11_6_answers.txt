1.
Relationship violence can occur in various kinds of personal relationships, such as between family members, partners, or spouses. It can happen to anyone and can take place in private or public spaces, involving individuals of different or the same gender identities. Relationship violence may also be referred to as domestic violence, intimate partner violence (IPV), dating violence, or family violence. It includes not only physical or sexual harm but also other forms of abuse where the abusive partner seeks to dominate and control the other through intimidation and fear.

2.
The potential warning signs of an abusive relationship could include:

- Intimidation and fear
- Dominating and controlling behavior
- Emotional abuse
- Isolation from friends and family
- Jealousy and possessiveness
- Constant criticism or belittling
- Threats of harm or actual harm
- Monitoring communication or movements
- Financial control or manipulation

3.
You can get help for relationship violence by contacting the Sexual Violence Support & Prevention Office (SVSPO) at 778.782.7233 or sv-support@sfu.ca. They offer personalized safety planning and referrals to both on and off-campus resources. Additionally, you can visit the Get Help pages for more information on campus and community support services.
