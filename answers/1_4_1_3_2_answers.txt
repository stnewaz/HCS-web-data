1.
The learning environment plays a crucial role in promoting student well-being at post-secondary institutions by impacting factors such as stress levels, social connectedness, engagement, and overall health. Positive classroom culture, course design, curriculum, assessment, and instructor support contribute to a healthy learning environment, which in turn fosters resilience, positive self-esteem, a sense of belonging, and empowerment among students. Additionally, it can enhance academic performance and retention by addressing mental health concerns and creating supportive conditions for students’ overall well-being.

2.
Student well-being positively impacts academic success and retention by enhancing learning experiences, fostering a sense of belonging, and reducing stress. It is closely tied to student retention, learning outcomes, and overall student experience. Improved mental health is correlated with better academic performance, and students who feel connected to their university are more likely to remain enrolled. Additionally, well-being concerns can negatively affect academic achievement, with stress linked to lower GPAs. Overall, a healthy learning environment that supports well-being contributes to student engagement, resilience, and long-term success.

3.
Students face significant and complex challenges regarding mental health during their college experience, including:

1. Increased anxiety and stress.
2. Social isolation.
3. Financial stress.
4. Increased academic load.
5. Feelings of loneliness and hopelessness.
6. Higher rates of depression.
7. The impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on mental well-being.
8. Difficulties related to the transition into higher education.
9. Greater stressors for international students and those from equity-deserving communities.

4.
Global events, such as the COVID-19 pandemic, have significantly influenced student mental health by increasing anxiety, social isolation, financial stress, and academic load. A study showed that 3 in 4 students reported experiencing negative mental health during their studies, with many students feeling very lonely, hopeless, and depressed. International students and those from equity-deserving communities have been particularly impacted by greater stressors affecting their mental well-being. The pandemic has highlighted the need to prioritize mental health and well-being programming, services, and initiatives in post-secondary education.

Classroom culture and course design can enhance student mental health by:

1. Fostering social connectedness and a sense of belonging, which positively impacts health and academic achievement.
2. Promoting positive self-esteem through supportive learning environments that encourage resilience and self-efficacy.
3. Providing opportunities for empowerment and autonomy, enhancing intrinsic motivation and well-being.
4. Encouraging engagement through meaningful contributions to society, which is beneficial for individual and collective well-being.
5. Educating students about healthy living and health behaviors, integrating personal and academic learning.
6. Building resilience by helping students cope with stressors in a positive way, mitigating negative consequences of stress.
7. Creating a positive classroom culture with instructor support, optimal challenges, and access to resources that contribute to overall well-being.

8.
Social connectedness contributes to student well-being by impacting various aspects of health, including health behavior choices, depression, anxiety, self-esteem, and overall longevity. It fosters a sense of belonging in the school community, which is protective of health and contributes to improved academic achievement and engagement. School connectedness has been shown to positively influence academic outcomes and is linked to better mental health.

9.
Self-esteem positively impacts self-rated health status, health-seeking behavior, health behavior choices, resilience, interpersonal relationships, academic achievement, and mental health. Higher self-esteem is linked to improved long-term health outcomes and contributes to psychosocial qualities such as self-efficacy, a sense of purpose, and social integration, which are important for student well-being.

10.
Autonomy is important for students in a learning environment because it contributes to enhanced intrinsic motivation, well-being, and improved individual functioning and resilience. A sense of autonomy and control is one of the psychological needs that support these positive outcomes. It fosters agency and empowers students, which can lead to better educational and learning experiences.

11.
Student engagement is closely linked to mental health and well-being in several ways:

1. **Social Connectedness**: Engagement fosters social support and connectedness, which positively impacts health behaviors, self-esteem, and academic achievement.

2. **Empowerment and Autonomy**: Engagement provides a sense of autonomy and control, which enhances intrinsic motivation and well-being.

3. **Resilience**: Actively engaging in learning and contributing to the community helps build resilience, enabling students to cope with stressors effectively.

4. **Positive Emotional States**: Engagement in meaningful activities is associated with positive emotions, which improve overall functioning, learning, and health.

5. **Academic Success**: Higher levels of engagement are correlated with better academic performance, which in turn contributes to improved mental health outcomes.

In summary, student engagement promotes a sense of belonging, autonomy, and emotional well-being, all of which are crucial for mental health and academic success.

18.
Positive emotional states are linked to improved overall functioning, learning, resilience, and health. Happy people tend to be healthier, more productive, and more socially engaged. Positive emotions broaden thought-action repertoires and build enduring personal resources, transforming individuals into more creative, knowledgeable, resilient, socially integrated, and healthy individuals.

19.
Educational institutions can address the mental health needs of international students by prioritizing mental health and well-being programming, services, and initiatives. They should create supportive classroom and campus environments that recognize the unique stressors faced by international students. Providing information and resources tailored to their needs, fostering social connectedness and belonging, promoting positive self-esteem, and enhancing engagement through curricular and extracurricular activities are essential. Additionally, creating opportunities for social interaction, instructor support, and access to resources can help improve their overall well-being and academic success. Institutions should also recognize the importance of resilience and healthy living education to empower students in managing their mental health.

20.
Poor mental health negatively impacts student retention rates by reducing students' sense of belonging and commitment to the institution, leading to increased dropout rates. Students experiencing mental health issues often report higher levels of stress and anxiety, which correlate with decreased academic performance and lower GPA. Additionally, poor mental health can hinder students' engagement and connection to their university community, further reducing their likelihood to remain enrolled. Overall, mental health is both an important input into student academic success and an outcome that affects students' decisions to persist in their studies.
