"Two-Spirit" is a term used by some Indigenous North American cultures to describe a person who embodies both masculine and feminine qualities, or who has a unique gender identity that is distinct from the binary male and female categories. It reflects a spiritual and cultural identity that encompasses various aspects of gender, sexuality, and community roles within Indigenous traditions.

Trans-informed pedagogy is an educational approach that acknowledges and incorporates the experiences and needs of transgender, gender non-binary, and gender diverse individuals. It involves creating an inclusive learning environment that respects and affirms diverse gender identities and expressions. 

To implement trans-informed pedagogy, consider the following strategies:

1. Use inclusive language and gender-neutral pronouns.
2. Incorporate diverse gender identities and perspectives in course materials.
3. Create a classroom atmosphere that encourages open discussions about gender.
4. Provide resources and support for transgender students.
5. Educate yourself and students about LGBTQ+ issues, including terminology and cultural awareness.
6. Allow for self-identification in surveys and class discussions.
7. Be mindful of the potential challenges faced by gender diverse students and offer accommodations as needed.
