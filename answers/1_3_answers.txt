My SSP is a service contracted by the university to provide support to all SFU students. It offers several options for graduate students, including booking appointments with a counsellor, chatting for advice through an app, and calling 24/7 for crisis support. Graduate students can use My SSP to manage stress, navigate relationships, and support others.

5.
You can contact Health & Counselling for an intake appointment, reach out to My SSP for support, or call or text 9-8-8 to reach the National Crisis and Suicide Line.

6.
You can access medical services on campus by booking appointments with doctors and nurses available for a range of medical services, advice, and care. Appointments can be scheduled for in-person visits at the Burnaby or Vancouver campus, and tele/virtual health visits are also available to accommodate your schedule and location.

7.
The Dissertation & Thesis Support Group is a safe and confidential space for graduate students writing their thesis or dissertation to share their experiences and receive support.

8.
Graduate students with disabilities can find support through various options, including connecting with the Centre for Accessible Learning and participating in a group designed to provide a safe space for seeking support, facilitated by a Registered Clinical Counsellor.

11.
Graduate students can learn about suicide awareness and prevention through the Suicide Awareness and Prevention Training, which provides a brief introduction on recognizing potential signs of distress and/or suicide risk, how to provide immediate support, and how to make appropriate referrals at SFU.

13.
1. Healthy Campus Community Initiatives - Over 300 faculty members and instructional staff are developing resources to foster well-being in learning environments, including in-class activities, teaching practices, and content to share with students.
2. Tools for Well-being in the Classroom - An initiative offering TAs and instructors strategies to support well-being in classes, tutorials, and learning environments.
3. Bouncing Forward Resilience Course - A free, online, self-paced course to build resilience and enhance well-being during graduate school.
4. Suicide Awareness and Prevention Training - Training designed to help participants recognize signs of distress or suicide risk and provide appropriate support and referrals.

14.
Graduate students can advocate for their needs with faculty members by learning to recognize and communicate their requirements effectively, utilizing the "Well-being through the Supervisory Process: Graduate Student Guide for Action," which provides a framework with five conditions for optimizing their learning experience. This includes understanding campus resources and fostering open dialogue with faculty to support their satisfaction, resilience, and success.

15.
The Faculty Guide to Action focuses on seven conditions informed by research, best practice, SFU faculty Champions, and graduate students to support graduate student well-being.
