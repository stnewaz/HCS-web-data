1.
Tech-facilitated sexualized violence (TFSV) is a specific type of sexualized violence that misuses technology to cause virtual and/or in-person harm. This can include, but is not limited to, misusing social media, virtual messaging platforms, webpages, dating apps, and digital, phone, or web cameras to threaten, harass, bully, embarrass, assault, extort, coerce, torment, or socially exclude another person.

2.
Women, Indigenous people, Black people, racialized communities, and 2SLGBTQI+ individuals.

3.
Some signs of tech-facilitated sexualized violence (TFSV) include:

- Misusing social media to threaten or harass
- Virtual messaging platforms used for bullying or extortion
- Dating apps exploited to coerce or socially exclude someone
- Digital, phone, or web cameras used to embarrass or assault
- Any behavior that causes virtual or in-person harm

4.
To report an incident of Tech-facilitated sexualized violence (TFSV), you can contact the Sexual Violence Support & Prevention Office (SVSPO) at 778.782.7233 or sv-support@sfu.ca. Additionally, consider reaching out to a trusted support person or one of the support services listed in the resources provided.

6.
The Intimate Images Protection Service is a confidential service established by the Government of British Columbia in 2023 to support people in B.C. who have had their intimate images shared without their consent. It provides specialists who can guide individuals through the steps to respond to threats of their images being released and how to address the situation if an image is released without consent.

12.
The Compass Tool is an online guide offered by the BC Society of Transition Houses that explains options and support for both threats and the release of intimate images. It can help individuals understand their options and navigate the support available to them. Users can also invite a friend or trusted person to go through the tool with them for support.
