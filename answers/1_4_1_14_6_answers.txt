Students have the opportunity to create a poster and talk about their research on a topic they care about during a poster session designed to meet educational goals. This event allows students to celebrate each other’s accomplishments and recognize their efforts in projects.

3.
The purpose of the poster session is to create an opportunity for students to share their projects, celebrate each other's accomplishments, and recognize their achievements in a supportive environment.

4.
Non-graded awards benefit students who struggle with quantitative analysis by acknowledging their creativity in conceptual development and effort in assignments. This inclusive approach helps these students feel recognized and valued for their contributions, even if they may not excel in quantitative skills. It reinforces the message that a variety of skills are important in conducting and communicating research, thereby supporting their overall well-being and encouraging their engagement in the learning process.

5.
Sample award categories include non-graded awards, awards for creativity in conceptual development, and recognition of effort in assignments.

6.
The awards process emphasizes the importance of diverse skills in research by recognizing creativity in conceptual development and effort in assignments, rather than solely focusing on quantitative analysis. It conveys a long-term message that various skills, beyond just quantitative skills, are vital for conducting and communicating quantitative research projects.
