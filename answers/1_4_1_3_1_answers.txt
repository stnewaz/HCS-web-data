6.
To assess student well-being in your class, consider using the following methods:

1. **Course Evaluations**: Incorporate specific questions related to well-being practices in your course evaluations to measure their impact on students.
2. **First Day and Midterm Questionnaires**: Use these to gather feedback from students about their needs and preferences, which can enhance the instructor-student relationship.
3. **Comment Cards**: Implement comment cards to receive ongoing feedback from students throughout the term.
4. **Reality Check Reflection Tool**: Encourage students to reflect on their values and abilities, which can provide insights into their well-being.
5. **Health and Well-being Resources Slide Deck**: Share information about available health and well-being resources at the beginning or end of your class.

To implement well-being check-ins in online learning environments, you can:

1. Embed brief well-being check-ins directly into your online course platform, such as Canvas.
2. Use simple prompts or questions to encourage students to share how they are feeling or any challenges they may be facing.
3. Schedule these check-ins regularly, such as at the beginning or end of each class or week.
4. Encourage students to reflect on their well-being in relation to their learning and provide a space for them to express their thoughts anonymously if preferred.
5. Incorporate the check-ins into discussions or activities to foster a sense of community and support among students.
