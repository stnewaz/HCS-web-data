Drug checking is a harm reduction service that allows people to determine the contents of their substances. It helps reduce risks associated with substance use by enabling individuals to identify potentially harmful substances, such as fentanyl, and take action to mitigate associated harms.

2.
You can access drug-checking services at all overdose prevention and supervised consumption sites, through Get Your Drugs Tested in Vancouver, or by mail.

3.
1. On-site fentanyl test strips
2. Take-home fentanyl test strips
3. FTIR Spectrometer used by a technician

4.
Fentanyl test strips help to identify whether or not there is fentanyl in your drug. They are labelled with "how to instructions" for people to refer to and provide a simple method for testing substances for the presence of fentanyl.

5.
An FTIR spectrometer is a device that provides more detailed drug checking information compared to fentanyl test strips. While fentanyl test strips help identify the presence of fentanyl in a substance, FTIR spectrometers offer a more comprehensive analysis of the drug's composition.
