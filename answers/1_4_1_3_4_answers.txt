12.
Feedback mechanisms play a vital role in creating a positive classroom culture by:

1. Encouraging open communication and dialogue between students and instructors.
2. Providing opportunities for students to express their thoughts and feelings about the course, leading to adjustments that meet their needs.
3. Helping to build a resilient and supportive learning community by fostering social connections among students.
4. Allowing instructors to understand what is working well and what needs improvement, creating a responsive learning environment.
5. Enhancing student engagement by involving them in the course design and expectations, thus reducing uncertainty.

Acknowledging the traditional territories of Indigenous peoples in the university context is significant as it demonstrates respect for the Indigenous communities and recognizes their historical and ongoing connections to the land. It fosters awareness of the impact of colonization, promotes reconciliation, and supports the inclusion of Indigenous perspectives within educational environments. This acknowledgment is an essential step in honoring Indigenous rights and cultures, contributing to a more inclusive and equitable academic community.
