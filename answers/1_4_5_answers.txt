1.
A Critical Incident, or Significant Event, is usually a traumatic experience that causes a strong emotional response, making it hard for a person to handle everyday tasks. It may negatively affect those involved and other community members.

2.
A Critical Incident can cause a strong emotional response that affects a person's ability to handle everyday tasks and may have a serious negative effect on those involved and other community members. It can lead to physical, emotional, cognitive, and behavioral responses. However, communities can be resilient and often have the resources to manage the impact without professional intervention, which helps build resilience and coping skills.

5.
A caring and healthy community supports members affected by Critical Incidents by responding with empathy and compassion. It encourages individuals to utilize their own resources to manage the impact of such events, helping them build resilience and coping skills. Additionally, the community can provide access to resources such as the Office of Student Support, Rights and Responsibilities, and various support services like Health and Counselling, Interfaith, and Security. Proper assessment and coordination of support options can be facilitated to ensure effective assistance.

9.
- Seek support from friends, family, or community members.
- Engage in self-care practices, such as exercise, meditation, or hobbies.
- Maintain a routine to create a sense of normalcy.
- Limit exposure to distressing news or discussions about the incident.
- Consider speaking to a mental health professional if needed.

10.
- Be respectful and confidential of others' private information.
- Do not share information unnecessarily.
- If concerned about someone's health, well-being, or safety, contact Campus Safety & Security, Health & Counselling Services, or the SFU Student Conduct Office.
- Consider escalating concerns to a supervisor.

11.

- Health & Counselling Services
- SFU Student Conduct Office

Critical Incident stress refers to the strong emotional response that individuals may experience following a traumatic event. This stress can impact their ability to manage everyday tasks and may negatively affect both the individuals involved and the broader community. Common responses to Critical Incident stress can be physical, emotional, cognitive/thinking, and behavioral.
