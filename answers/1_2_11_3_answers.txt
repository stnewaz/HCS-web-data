Opioids are a family of drugs that are usually prescribed to relieve pain, control coughs, and treat diarrhea. Examples include:

1. **Natural Opioids**: 
   - Codeine (in Tylenol No. 2, No. 3, and No. 4)
   - Morphine

2. **Semi-synthetic Opioids**: 
   - Hydromorphone (Dilaudid)
   - Oxycodone

3. **Synthetic Opioids**: 
   - Methadone
   - Buprenorphine

Medical uses include:
- Relieving acute or chronic pain
- Controlling moderate to severe coughs
- Treating diarrhea
- Treating addiction to other opioids.

15.
Naloxone is a medication that can temporarily reverse the effects of an opioid overdose and allow time for medical help to arrive.

16.
Potential risks of using prescription opioids include:

1. Addiction and abuse.
2. Long-term use can cause constipation, decreased interest in sex, menstrual irregularities, and mood swings.
3. Physical dependence leading to withdrawal symptoms.
4. Withdrawal symptoms can include anxiety, insomnia, and drug craving.
5. Dangerous if used without medical supervision.

17.
Symptoms of opioid withdrawal include anxiety, insomnia, drug craving, and may include other symptoms that usually subside after a week, although some symptoms may continue for a long time. Opioid withdrawal is rarely life-threatening.

18.
To safely use opioids, one should:

1. Follow medical advice and use as prescribed by a licensed practitioner.
2. Avoid using opioids for reasons other than their medical purpose.
3. Be aware of the risk of addiction, especially if there is a personal or family history of substance abuse.
4. Regularly communicate with healthcare providers about the use and effects of the medication.
5. Have access to a naloxone kit for emergency situations.
6. Understand the potential side effects and risks associated with long-term use.
7. Gradually taper off the medication under medical supervision if discontinuing use.
