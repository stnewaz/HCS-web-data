1.
The purpose of the self-paced online module offered by SFU is to explore the realities and impact of sexualized violence, examine the factors that contribute to its presence in communities, learn to recognize harmful behaviors, understand the role of consent, and gain practical tools to safely intervene. It aims to support individuals in empowering themselves, supporting friends, and becoming proactive allies.

- Supporting friends and being there for others affected by sexualized violence

5.
Participating in the module can benefit someone looking to support a friend by equipping them with knowledge and confidence to take meaningful action. They will learn how to recognize harmful behaviors, understand the role of consent, gain practical tools to safely intervene, and find support for themselves and for others affected by sexualized violence.

The module helps individuals become proactive allies by equipping them with knowledge and confidence to take meaningful action, teaching them how to recognize harmful behaviors, understand the role of consent, and providing practical tools to safely intervene when needed. It also informs them about where to find support and how to assist others affected by sexualized violence.

10.
The final learning reflection in the module is significant because it is required to receive 1.5 hours of Co-Curricular Record (CCR) recognition. Completing this reflection ensures that the module will show up on the student's CCR in the term after completion.

11.
The Safer Campuses recognition will appear on your Co-Curricular Record in the term after you complete the module (e.g., if you complete the module in September, it will show up on your CCR in the Spring Term).
