My SSP is a resource that provides 24/7 crisis support. It allows you to call or chat to schedule an appointment with a counsellor in your community, including options for counsellors with similar lived experiences. It is recommended to have the app on your phone for easy access when needed.

To obtain documentation for CAL, you can have your own General Practitioner, Psychologist, or Psychiatrist complete the necessary forms. This is recommended as they will know you better if they have been seeing you for a while. If you are unable to book with your own healthcare provider, you can book with a doctor at a Health & Counselling clinic.

10.
If you need accommodations but are not registered with the Centre for Accessible Learning (CAL), you can still reach out for support. Here are options available:

1. Contact the CAL for guidance on accessing support without registration.
2. Explore if you can obtain documentation from your General Practitioner, Psychologist, or Psychiatrist to facilitate your registration with CAL.
3. If you lack access to healthcare and documentation, inquire about other support services available on campus.
4. Consider reaching out to student-led organizations, such as the SFU Disability and Neurodiversity Alliance (DNA), for advocacy and assistance.
