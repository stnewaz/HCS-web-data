3.
Academic concessions are granted at the discretion of individual instructors and/or in accordance with established departmental or Faculty policies and procedures. Students should speak to their instructor as a first step. Concessions may take the form of extensions, deferred exams, make-up exams, alternate assignments, or re-weighting of grades. Students are required to complete the Academic Concession Form when seeking concessions. Instructors may request medical documentation for certain situations.

4.
Academic concessions are granted at the discretion of individual instructors in response to unexpected situations that prevent student participation in course-related activities, while academic accommodations are required modifications or additions of University resources to ensure fair access and prevent discrimination based on protected grounds, such as a disability.

5.
To obtain an academic accommodation at SFU, a student should:

1. Identify the need for an accommodation due to barriers to fair access to learning opportunities (e.g., disabilities, religious conflicts).
2. Contact the SFU Centre for Accessible Learning for assistance, especially for disability-related accommodations.
3. The Centre will notify the instructor about the type of accommodation required.
4. For religious accommodations, the student should follow guidance from the SFU Human Rights Office.
5. Ensure that documentation is provided when necessary, as required by university policies.

8.
A DE grade is a temporary grade assigned at the end of the term for incomplete coursework. It is issued in two circumstances: when a student has not completed coursework by the end of the term, and it will eventually revert to a letter grade or notation. All unchanged DE notations will be converted automatically to N grades after the end of the first week in the following term, unless an extension is granted by the instructor.

9.
A student can request an extension for a DE grade by having the request approved by the instructor and the department chair, and submitting it in writing to the Office of the Registrar with a final deferral date. Normally, the maximum extension allowed is the end of the term following the original deferral.

10.
The WE notation indicates that a student applied for a WE and had their application approved, allowing them to drop a course after the normal course drop deadline due to extenuating circumstances.

11.
Students apply for a WE through the Academic Appeals office in Student Services and not through their instructors. Approval is not guaranteed.

12.
An AE grade, sometimes called a “compassionate pass,” is granted by the Registrar acting on the recommendation of the instructor or department chair for an incomplete course on medical or compassionate grounds. It is generally reserved for extreme cases where instructor-granted concessions, DE, or WE options are not available, and normally the course requirements will have been substantially fulfilled. Written evidence must support the request for the AE grade, and this evidence must be received within 96 hours of a scheduled final exam or the last day of term lectures for which such standing is requested. Courses for which aegrotat standing is granted are not included in the GPA calculation.
